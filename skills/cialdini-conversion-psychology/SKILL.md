# Robert Cialdini - Conversion Psychology

The practical application layer of Cialdini's 7 principles — use these frameworks when building, reviewing, or optimizing landing pages, email sequences, sales calls, pricing pages, and objection handling. Score every conversion asset against these principles and rewrite weak elements.

**Routes when user asks about:** conversion optimization, landing page copy, email sequences, sales scripts, pricing psychology, objection handling, CRO, funnel optimization, why people don't buy, checkout optimization, free trial conversion, sales psychology

## How to Use This Skill

When building or reviewing any conversion asset, apply the relevant section below. Score each element, identify the weakest principles, and strengthen them with specific copy/design changes. Every conversion moment should activate at least 3 of the 7 principles.

## Landing Page Persuasion Framework

Score each element 1-5 when reviewing or building a landing page.

### Above the Fold (first screen, no scroll)

**Headline** — Must activate a Pain/Desire or Social Proof signal:
- Weak: "The Best Project Management Tool"
- Strong: "The project management tool that 14,000 remote teams switched to when Jira got too complex"
- Score criteria: Specific? Customer-language? Benefit-first? Social proof embedded?

**Subheadline** — Must handle the #1 objection or elaborate the #1 benefit:
- Weak: "Simple, powerful, beautiful"
- Strong: "Get your team set up in 10 minutes — no training, no migration headache"
- Score criteria: Addresses real fear? Specific? Credible?

**CTA (above fold)** — Must be low-commitment, specific, and scarcity-aware:
- Weak: "Sign Up"
- Strong: "Start Free — No Credit Card Required"
- Score criteria: Low barrier? Removes fear? Clear next step?

**Hero visual** — Must show product in use by someone like the prospect:
- Weak: Stock photo of people at a whiteboard
- Strong: Screenshot of actual product with real data visible
- Score criteria: Realistic? Aspirational outcome visible? Customer-type recognizable?

### Social Proof Section

**Logos**: Only recognizable ones. Unrecognizable logos signal low authority.

**Testimonials** — Score each on 3 dimensions (1-5):

| Dimension | Weak (1) | Strong (5) |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Similarity to prospect | Generic "business owner" | Specific "Series A SaaS founder" |
| Specificity of result | "Really helped us" | "Increased ARR by $2.3M in 6 months" |
| Credibility of source | Anonymous | Named person, company, photo |

Below 9/15: rewrite or replace.

**Numbers**: "Used by 12,400 companies" (specific, not round) > "Thousands." Best: "14,000 remote teams have saved an average of 4.2 hours/week" (result embedded).

### Objection Handling Section

Every landing page must address top 3 objections before the CTA. Identify them from sales call recordings, support tickets, and exit surveys.

| Objection Type | Signal | How to Address |
|---------------|--------|---------------|
| "Will it work for me?" | Specificity doubt | Case studies featuring similar customers |
| "Is this trustworthy?" | Trust deficit | Third-party logos, press, credentials |
| "Is it worth the price?" | Value doubt | ROI calculation, comparison to alternative |
| "Is it too complex?" | Effort fear | "Set up in 10 minutes" + proof |
| "Do I need it now?" | Urgency deficit | Scarcity/deadline or cost-of-inaction framing |

## Email Sequence Blueprints

Map each email in a sequence to a specific Cialdini principle.

### Welcome Sequence (5 emails over 10 days)

**Email 1 — Reciprocity**: Deliver value immediately, no ask.
- Subject: "Your [free resource/tool/insight] is ready"
- Body: Pure value — framework, template, or insight usable today
- No CTA other than "reply if you have questions"
- Goal: trigger reciprocity before any sales ask

**Email 2 — Authority + Social Proof**: Establish credibility.
- Subject: "How [similar company] achieved [specific result]"
- Body: Case study matching the prospect's situation
- Subtle CTA: "Curious if this could work for you?"

**Email 3 — Commitment Trigger**: Small yes before big yes.
- Subject: "Quick question about your goals"
- Body: Ask them to state their goal. Written commitment to a goal increases purchase likelihood.

**Email 4 — Liking**: Show the human behind the brand.
- Subject: "The mistake I made that led to [company name]"
- Body: Founder story with genuine vulnerability. Shift from "product" to "person I trust."

**Email 5 — Scarcity + CTA**: The ask.
- Subject: "Last chance for [offer]" or "Before you miss this"
- Body: Reiterate value, introduce genuine scarcity (trial expiring, cohort closing, price increasing). Hard CTA — clear, specific, one action.

### Re-engagement Sequence

**Email 1 — Reciprocity**: New value, no ask. "We thought you'd find this useful."
**Email 2 — Social Proof + FOMO**: Feature updates + customer results.
**Email 3 — Personal outreach**: Plain text, first name only in subject. "I noticed you haven't started — anything I can help with?"
**Email 4 — Honest goodbye**: "This is my last email for a while." Reverse psychology via consistency — saying goodbye triggers engagement from people who don't want to be written off.

## Sales Call Framework (Influence-Mapped)

### Opening (0-5 min): Liking + Unity
- Genuine interest: "Before I start, tell me about your situation" — listen for 3+ minutes
- Find genuine connection or similarity
- Mirror their language for how you describe the product later

### Discovery (5-20 min): Commitment Trigger via Questions
Ask questions that lead them to articulate their own pain and goals:
- "What's the #1 challenge you're trying to solve right now?"
- "What would ideal look like?"
- "What would achieving that mean for your team?"

When they describe pain in detail, they've committed to having the problem. When they describe the ideal, they've committed to wanting it.

### Demo (20-40 min): Authority + Social Proof
- Show features that directly address their stated pain (not a generic tour)
- For each feature: "This is where [similar company] saved 3 hours per week doing X"
- Social proof from customers matching their exact situation
- Authority through specifics: "We ran 200 implementations; here's the pattern we always see"

### Handling Objections (40-50 min): Consistency + Reciprocity
1. Validate genuinely (liking and rapport)
2. Reference their earlier commitment: "You mentioned [goal] was critical — how does [objection] weigh against that?"
3. Give information, not pressure
4. If they need to think: "What information would help you make this decision?" — keeps commitment loop

### Close (50-60 min): Scarcity + Small Commitment
- Never ask "want to sign up?" — too large
- Ask for next smallest yes: "Would it make sense to do a pilot with your team for 30 days?"
- Name honest scarcity if it exists. If it doesn't, don't invent it.

## Pricing Page Psychology

### Anchoring
Show highest tier first (or most prominently) to anchor all subsequent prices. **Decoy pricing**: three tiers where the middle is clearly best value — most buyers choose the middle.

### The Goldilocks Effect
Three options → most choose the middle. Structure so middle tier is your preferred sale. Highlight with "Most Popular" or "Best Value" badge. Never name a tier "Basic" — nobody wants to be basic.

### Price Presentation
- Annual as monthly equivalent: "$39/month billed annually" > "$468/year"
- Show savings explicitly: "Save $156/year" — loss aversion makes savings feel like avoiding loss
- Per-user pricing: "$8/user/month" feels trivial vs. "$800/month for 100 users"

### Risk Reversal
Every pricing page needs:
- Money-back guarantee (remove buyer's remorse risk)
- Free trial (commitment without risk)
- Cancel anytime (no lock-in anxiety)

Prominent guarantees increase conversion more than the refunds they cost.

## Objection Handling Through Influence Principles

### "It's too expensive."
**Principles**: Anchoring + Social Proof + Reciprocity
- Anchor upward: "Most companies pay 3x this for a custom solution"
- ROI reframe: "At your volume, this pays for itself in [time period]"
- Social proof: "Companies similar to yours report saving [amount] monthly"
- Give first: "Let me show you the ROI calculator before you decide"

### "I need to think about it."
**Principles**: Commitment + Consistency + Scarcity
- Reference their stated goals: "You mentioned [goal] was critical — what would help you feel confident?"
- Small commitment: "What if we did a 2-week pilot?"
- Honest scarcity: name real deadlines if they exist
- Never: "Take your time" (eliminates urgency entirely)

### "We're already using [competitor]."
**Principles**: Social Proof + Authority + Liking
- Validate current choice (don't criticize competitor — violates liking principle)
- Peer proof: "Many customers switched from [competitor] — here's what drove the change"
- Specificity: concrete functional difference, not generic superiority claims
- Small yes: "Would a 15-minute comparison be worth it?"

### "Now isn't the right time."
**Principles**: Loss Aversion + Commitment + Scarcity
- Cost of inaction: "What does [problem] cost you per month?"
- Reference goals: "How does waiting 6 months affect [goal]?"
- Gentle scarcity if real: "Pricing/capacity changes on [date]"
- Small commitment: "Can we set a check-in for when timing is right?"

## The Pre-Purchase Checklist

Run every conversion moment through this before launching:

**Reciprocity**
- [ ] Have we given value before asking for the conversion?
- [ ] Is the free offering genuinely valuable?

**Scarcity**
- [ ] Is there a genuine reason to act now?
- [ ] Is the scarcity real and communicated honestly?

**Authority**
- [ ] Is credibility established before the CTA?
- [ ] Are claims specific (numbers, names, results)?

**Social Proof**
- [ ] Are testimonials from people similar to the prospect?
- [ ] Are testimonials specific (results, not just sentiment)?

**Commitment**
- [ ] Is the first ask small and low-risk?
- [ ] Have we created prior micro-commitments?

**Liking**
- [ ] Does the copy sound like a human, not a corporation?
- [ ] Is there a founder or team story that creates connection?

**Unity**
- [ ] Does the prospect feel part of a community?
- [ ] Is there shared identity language?
